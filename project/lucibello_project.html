New York Mets History

About:

The New York Mets are an American professional baseball team in Major League Baseball’s National League East division. The Mets were one of baseballs first expansion teams in 1962, bring national league baseball back to New York after the Brooklyn Dodgers and New York Giants moved to Los Angeles and San Francisco respectively. The Mets play in the National Leauge Eastern Division alongside the Atlanta Brave, Philadelphia Phillies, Washington Nationals, and Miami Marlins.

Going into the 2021 season there are high hopes for the Mets. In the offseason the team has signed top free agent pitcher Max Scherzer on a record setting deal. The team also signed All-Star Center Fielder Starling Marte, 1st Baseman/Outfielder Mark Cahna, and 3rd Baseman Eduardo Escobar. Fans wait day by day for the offseason to end, spring training to come, and Mets baseball to resume. Mets fans often get their hopes up but maybe 2021 will be a good year to remember filled with joy and a World Series trophy.

How did the Mets become a franchise?

  • After the Brooklyn Dodgers and New York Giants relocated to California, there was a strong interest from fans in having National League baseball back in New York. William Shea, who the team’s stadium from 1964-2008 is named after, was the primary force in bringing baseball back to New York.
  • With interest from former minority owners of the Giants, and other former owners of New York clubs, the National League and American League decided to expand with two new teams in each league, one of which would be in New York.

Why are they called the Mets?

  • When deciding on team names before the league expansions, the New York based team had 10 finalists for possible names. These names included the Avengers, Bees, Burros, Continentals, Jets, Mets, NYBS, Rebels, Skyliners, and Skyscrapers.
  • The Mets were ultimately decided upon in part because of a different historical baseball team, The New York Metropolitans, who played from 1883 to 1888.

Homes:

Polo Grounds:

  • 1962-1963
  • The Polo Grounds hosted the following sports: baseball, football, college football, soccer, gaelic football, boxing, and motorsports.
  • Fun Fact: The Center Field fence was 483 ft away.

Shea Stadium:

  • 1964-2008
  • Shea installed the iconic Home Run Apple in 1980.
  • Fun Fact: The Beatles opened their 1965 North American tour at Shea Stdium with a record attendance of 55,600.

Citi Field:

  • 2009-present
  • Citi Field's exterior is designed with inspiration from the former home of the Brooklyn Dodgers: Ebbits Field
  • Fun Fact: There is a bridge in deep right center field named after Shea Stadium
