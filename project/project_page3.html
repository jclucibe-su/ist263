Page 3 - Mets Fan Favorite Players

Tom Seaver:

Short Biography: Tom Seaver, a.k.a “Tom Terrific” is the greatest Met to ever have the honor to put on the uniform. Born November 17, 1944, in Fresno, California, Tom would play college ball at the University of Southern California before getting drafted originally by the Dodgers in 1965. Seaver would begin his Mets career just two years later in 1967 at the age of 22. Seaver would go on to become one of the greatest pitchers in the history of baseball, winning 311 games, striking out 3,640 batters, and winning three Cy Young awards along the way. Seaver led the Mets to a World Series victory in 1969 and became a hero in Queens for decades to come.

  • July 9, 1969: came within two outs of a perfect game against the Chicago Cubs. Threw five one-hitters during career.

Mike Piazza:

Short Biography: Mike Piazza is the second player to be inducted into baseball’s Hall of Fame wearing a Mets uniform. Mike famously was drafted in the 62nd round of the 1988 amateur draft as a favor by Dodgers Legend Tommy Lasorda. Piazza was able to defy the odds and go on to become a baseball Hall of Famer, lead the Mets to the 2000 World Series, and become a fan favorite.
